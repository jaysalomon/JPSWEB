The AV1 Retina

Repurposing commodity video codec hardware as ultra-low-power sensory preprocessors for machine vision.

The Raw Data Bottleneck

Modern vision systems process video frame-by-frame, pixel-by-pixel. Every frame, billions of floating-point operations recalculate spatial and temporal relationships from scratch.

4K @ 60fps = 500M pixels/sec
Each pixel processed 100+ times → 50 GFLOP/s wasted

The Codec Already Does This

AV1 video encoders already extract the features vision networks need: motion vectors, edge maps, temporal deltas. This silicon exists in every modern SoC—and it's sitting idle.

Motion Estimation → Object Tracking
CDEF Edges → Boundary Detection
Temporal Prediction → Change Awareness

Core Insight

"The retina doesn't send pixels to the brain. It sends features. AV1 can do the same for silicon."

The biological retina preprocesses light before it ever reaches the visual cortex—extracting edges, motion, and contrast locally. AV1 hardware performs structurally identical operations during video encoding. We propose hijacking this pipeline.

Feature Extraction Pipeline

What AV1 computes that vision networks pay dearly for:

Motion Vectors

Block-level displacement fields tracking how content moves between frames. Directly usable for object tracking and optical flow.

CDEF Edge Maps

Constrained Directional Enhancement Filter detects and enhances perceptually important edges—the very boundaries vision networks learn to find.

Temporal Residuals

Difference signals between predicted and actual frames. Highlights change—exactly what anomaly detection and attention systems need.
