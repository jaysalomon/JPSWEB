TECHNICAL WHITEPAPER SUMMARY • DEC 2025

The Unified Mind Space

Resolving the "Topological Crisis" of modern AI by bridging stable crystallised knowledge with plastic fluid intelligence.

The Problem: Topological Rigidity

Current LLMs suffer from the Engineer's Paradox. They are "frozen city grids" — optimisation for specific routes (tasks) makes building new roads (learning novel logic) impossible without retraining.

  • Static Computational Graphs
  • Inability to reason abductively
  • Requires "patches" like Chain-of-Thought

The Solution: Morphogenesis

UMS introduces Topological Plasticity. The network physically grows new "Relational Processors" to handle entropy, then consolidates them into the backbone.

  • Dynamic Growth (Morphogenesis)
  • Lifelong Learning without forgetting
  • Separation of Concept & Relation

Theoretical Foundations

Coupled Manifolds

Cognition is split into two distinct, interacting spaces.

Principal Manifold (P)

Stores static concepts (crystallised intelligence). Like the Neocortex.

Relational Manifold (R)

Dynamic operators acting on concepts (fluid intelligence). Like the Hippocampus.

The Levi Graph Transformation

Silicon is based on pairwise graphs, but thoughts are hypergraphs (one relation connecting many concepts). The Levi Transform bridges this by "reifying" relations into concrete nodes.

System 2 Dynamics: Stable Growth

1. The Growth Signal

G(x) = Entropy + Divergence

When System 1 is confused (High Entropy) or wrong (High Divergence), a signal is fired to spawn a new processor.

2. mHC Stability

Birkhoff Polytope

New connections are constrained to doubly stochastic matrices. This mathematically guarantees signal energy never explodes.

3. Cis-Elasticity

Topological Penalty

New branches act like rubber bands. They stretch to learn new data but are anchored to the backbone to prevent catastrophic drift.

The Learning Lifecycle

High-Plasticity Morphogenesis

The system is alert. When novel data arrives, System 1 stays frozen while System 2 dynamically spawns new branches to adapt. This is "fluid intelligence" in action.

  • Growth Signal triggers on high entropy.
  • New Relational Processors are created instantly.
  • System 1 Backbone acts as a stable anchor.
